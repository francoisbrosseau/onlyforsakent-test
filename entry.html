The Castle Remembers

The door shuts without a sound—yet the air grows heavier, as if the stone itself has been holding its breath for centuries.

Ahead: arches drowned in soot, prayer-candles still warm, and a corridor that shouldn’t exist. Each step feels borrowed. Each heartbeat feels counted.

You came for the relic they buried in the dark—an ancient gem with a name that was erased from every tongue that spoke it. They say it doesn’t shine. It stares.

If you want it, you will not be guided by signs or maps. Only by what flickers at the edge of your vision.

So it knows your name.
