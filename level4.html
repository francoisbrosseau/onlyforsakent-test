Castle Descent • Level 4

The Hall of Whispering Stone

The statue is behind you now. The castle does not feel larger — it feels closer. Something remembers you… and it wants you to keep going.

The stairs spiral down far longer than they should.

Your footsteps echo, but the echo returns a fraction too late, as if something is repeating them after you. The air grows colder, thicker, tasting of dust and old iron.

At the bottom, the stairwell opens into a vast corridor carved directly from black rock. No torches. No windows. Yet you can see.

The walls are covered with shapes that almost look like faces, stretching outward from the stone, mouths open in silent warnings. Their eyes follow you as you walk.

Halfway down the hall, you begin to hear it.

Whispers. Not loud enough to understand, but layered — desperate, overlapping. Some beg. Some laugh. Some repeat the same word again and again.

Above a door of pale marble, letters slowly carve themselves into the surface, as if written by an invisible claw. The whispers stop. One voice remains.

“If you wish to go further, you must answer.
Speak the thing that is taken from you the moment you try to hold it.”
Stone Notes (Do not read aloud)
Look at the first letter of each marked line. They are not marked by accident.

Beneath every whisper, the corridor drinks what you give it.

Remember: the marble does not open for steel or flame.

Each step steals warmth, as if the stone is alive.

Air turns thin when fear becomes curiosity.

Try to clutch it, and it slips through your fingers instantly.

Hold it too long… and the castle holds you back.

Riddle

“What is taken from you the moment you try to hold it?”

The Marble Door

Enter the password to descend. The corridor listens. (Tip: passwords are not case-sensitive.)

The door remains sealed. The faces in the walls begin to smile again.

It opens.

A cold draft spills out from the seam — not a breeze, but a slow exhale. For the first time, the whispers sound almost… satisfied.

Beyond the door, the staircase descends into a darker rhythm, as if the castle has a heartbeat.
