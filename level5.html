Chapter V — The Mark That Binds

The Sigil Chamber

The corridor exhales into a circular room carved with spirals that never quite repeat. A shallow basin sits at the center—bone-dry—yet the air tastes like wet stone. Above the basin, tiles hover in a slow orbit, each etched with a single letter that flickers like a living ember.

On the far door, a symbol is scratched deep into the metal. Not a word—an instruction. A warning. A promise. The carving seems to shift when you look away, as if the room is trying to remember what you are.

A whisper drifts from the walls: “Set the mark in order… and the mark will set you.”

Riddle

I am not a name, yet I claim you.
Draw me once, and doors obey.
I am a mark that speaks without sound—
a secret shape that binds the way.
What am I?
