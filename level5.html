ONLYFORSAKEN

Level VI — The Hall of Repeat

Chapter VI: The corridor tightens as if the castle has begun to breathe.

You pass a doorway that you swear you’ve already passed—its iron handle bent like a broken finger, the same scrape in the stone beneath it, the same stain that never dries. The deeper you walk, the more the architecture stops obeying distance. Steps become suggestions. Corners return you to places you never meant to revisit.

There is a sound now, too—soft, almost polite. Not footsteps behind you… but answers. Your own breathing comes back to you with a delay, as if the walls are learning it. The castle repeats you, not to mock you— but to measure you.

At the end of the hall, a niche holds a slab of black stone. No lock. No keyhole. Only a shallow indentation where a word should be placed like an offering. Above it, etched into the mortar, a warning:

Riddle — The Silent Judge
I speak without a mouth.
I answer without a voice.
I follow you longer than shadows.
What am I?
Tip: answers here are a single word. No spaces. No punctuation.
If you speak it right, the castle speaks you back.
